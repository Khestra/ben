About Me

My name is Aboagye Nyamekeh Prince, and I come from the vibrant and culturally rich country of Ghana. From an early age, I have been fascinated by the power of technology and its ability to transform lives. This passion led me to pursue Computer Science and Engineering at the University of Connecticut, where I am continuously honing my skills in software development, problem-solving, and system design.

My journey in tech has been a mixture of curiosity, hard work, and endless learning. I currently work as a Backend Engineer Intern at Feel the Industry, where I develop scalable applications that improve efficiency in virtual health consultations. At the same time, I serve as an IT Help Assistant at UConn, assisting students and faculty with technology-related issues. These experiences have allowed me to bridge the gap between technical expertise and real-world problem-solving.

Outside of my academic and professional life, I have a deep love for coding, artificial intelligence, and cybersecurity. I enjoy working on projects that challenge me, from building secure password managers to creating tournament scoring systems that can handle real-time data. Programming, for me, is more than just writing code; it is a tool to create, innovate, and bring ideas to life.

Beyond technology, I find joy in gaming, especially competitive tournaments where strategy and skill come into play. I also have an appreciation for music, particularly Afrobeat, which keeps me motivated during long hours of coding. However, I am not a fan of slow internet, poorly documented code, or extreme cold weather, all of which can significantly test my patience.

I strongly believe in continuous learning and collaboration. The world of technology is evolving at an incredible pace, and I am eager to be part of this transformation. Whether it is through open-source contributions, tech meetups, or online communities, I am always looking to connect with like-minded individuals who share my enthusiasm for innovation.

If you would like to see my work or connect with me, feel free to check out my projects on GitHub or reach out to me on LinkedIn. I am always open to discussions about technology, future opportunities, and exciting ideas that can shape the future.
